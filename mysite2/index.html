Free Trial Swimming Lesson for Children

A trial lesson is an individual or mini-group session with a coach.
It takes place in the family’s pool or at an agreed location, with the mandatory presence of a parent or legal guardian.

  • Introduce the child to the coach in a calm, familiar environment.
  • Assess the child’s skill level and confidence in the water.
  • Understand whether the lesson format and coaching approach are a good fit.
  • Discuss parents’ goals and expectations before starting regular training.
  • Use game-based elements — no pressure, no rush.
  • Provide a basic skills assessment.
  • Help the child adapt to the water comfortably and confidently.

Individual Lesson

An Individual Lesson is a one-on-one session with a coach, where all attention is focused on the child: their level, personality, pace, and goals.
We don’t adjust the child to fit the program — we adjust the program to fit the child.
What’s included in an individual lesson?
45 minutes – in the water
Teaching and practicing swimming skills
Technique work tailored to age and level
Playful elements for motivation
Gentle correction of mistakes without pressure
Building confidence and independence in the water
10–12 minutes – out of the water
Light stretching and preparation for the lesson
Getting to know the child (for first sessions)
Discussing the goals and objectives of the lesson
Explaining what we will do today and why
Reviewing mistakes and giving recommendations after the lesson
Answering parents’ questions
Parents always understand what we are working on and the child’s progress.

Family Group Lesson – Up to 4 Children

A Family Group Lesson is designed for children from the same family (brothers, sisters) or close relatives, where learning takes place in a comfortable, friendly atmosphere under the constant attention of the coach.
This format helps children feel more confident, while parents can observe the process and track each child’s progress.
Total duration: approximately 55–60 minutes
45 minutes – in the water
Teaching and developing swimming skills
Exercises adapted to each child’s level
Playful elements and group activities

Individual tips and gentle correction of mistakes
Even in a group, every child receives personalized attention.
10–12 minutes – out of the water
Getting acquainted and setting the tone for the lesson
Explaining the training plan
Considering each child’s individual characteristics
Reviewing mistakes and providing recommendations after the lesson
Answering parents’ questions

Group Lesson – Up to 10 Children

A Group Lesson for up to 10 children is a format for joint training in a rented pool, where children practice together in a safe, organized, and motivating environment under the guidance of a coach.
Lessons take place only with an established, consistent group.
Lesson duration: approximately 60 minutes
In the water:
Group warm-up and water adaptation
Technique and coordination exercises
Tasks adapted for different skill levels
Playful elements and team motivation
Constant supervision and guidance by the coach
This format is suitable for children who already feel confident in the water.
Where do the lessons take place?
Lessons are held in a rented pool that accommodates up to 10 children and meets all safety requirements.
The pool is booked in advance with parents’ agreement.
Location and schedule are confirmed after the group is formed.
Lessons are conducted on a regular basis, not as a one-time session.
Children also have the opportunity to participate in friendly competitions to practice skills and celebrate progress.
Payment methods accepted: Zelle, PayPal, Square
